# Low Velocity Wind Energy Harvesting Using Vibration Method

## Overview

This project presents a fully mechanical design for harvesting low velocity wind energy through vibration-based methods. The system leverages two key aerodynamic phenomena to generate electrical energy from wind that would otherwise be wasted.

## Key Technologies

### Vortex Induced Vibration (VIV)
- Exploits oscillations caused by vortex shedding from cylindrical structures
- Occurs when wind flows across a bluff body (typically a cylinder)
- Creates rhythmic vibrations at the natural frequency of the structure

### Turbulence Induced Vibration (TIV)
- Harnesses vibrations caused by turbulent wind patterns
- More effective in complex wind environments
- Captures energy from random fluctuations in wind flow

## Project Goals

- Design a purely mechanical system (no complex electronics required)
- Optimize energy harvesting from low velocity wind (< 10 m/s)
- Create a scalable, durable, and cost-effective solution
- Minimize maintenance requirements

## Features

- **Low Wind Speed Operation**: Designed to function efficiently in low velocity wind conditions
- **Mechanical Design**: Fully mechanical approach without complex control systems
- **Dual Method Integration**: Combines both VIV and TIV mechanisms for improved efficiency
- **Scalability**: Can be designed for various scales and applications
